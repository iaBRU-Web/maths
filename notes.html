GROUP 18 ELEMENTS (NOBLE GASES)

13.1 — What Are Noble Gases?

Group 18: He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe, Rn. All have completely filled valence shells (He: 1s²; others: ns²np⁶ — the octet structure). Because their outer shells are full, they do not gain, lose, or share electrons → very unreactive → called noble gases.

13.2 — Physical Properties

  • Monoatomic, colourless, odourless, tasteless.
  • Very low melting/boiling points; sparingly soluble in water.
  • Very high ionization energies — decrease ↓ down the group as atomic size increases.
  • Helium has the lowest boiling point of any substance: −269°C.

13.3 — Occurrence

  • Found in atmosphere (0.03% total; argon most abundant). All except radon.
  • He and Ne also in radioactive minerals (pitchblende, monazite).
  • Radon: decay product of ²²⁶Ra. Xenon and Radon are the rarest.

13.4 — Reactivity / Inertness

Only Kr and Xe form compounds. First compound made in 1962 by Neil Bartlett:

Xe + PtF₆ → XePtF₆  (first noble gas compound)
Xe + 2F₂ → XeF₄  (400°C, 6 atm, nickel vessel)
  • XeF₂, XeF₄, XeF₆ — colourless crystalline solids; powerful fluorinating agents.
  • 2XeF₂ + 2H₂O → 2Xe + 4HF + O₂  |  2XeF₆ + 3H₂O → XeO₃ + 6HF
  • He and Ne form NO compounds — too small, ionization energy too high.

13.5 — Uses of Noble Gases

GasUses

PERIOD 3 ELEMENTS — PROPERTIES & TRENDS

Period 3 (left → right): Na, Mg, Al, Si, P, S, Cl, Ar

14.1 — Physical Property Trends

Ionization Energy — INCREASES → (exceptions: Al < Mg; S < P)

  • Al < Mg: Al⁺ forms stable full 3s² after losing 3p¹ electron.
  • S < P: S⁺ forms more stable half-full 3p³ (S has a paired 3p electron that repels).

Atomic Radius — DECREASES →

More protons, same shell, no extra shielding → stronger nuclear pull → smaller atom.

Electronegativity — INCREASES → (Ar = 0, no bonds)

Stronger nuclear charge + smaller radius → stronger attraction of shared electrons.

Melting & Boiling Points

  • Na, Mg, Al (metals): increases Na < Mg < Al — more delocalized electrons (+1, +2, +3 charges).
  • Si (giant covalent, like diamond): very high melting point (~1683 K).
  • P, S, Cl, Ar (simple molecules): low m.p. — only weak van der Waals forces. Order: S₈ > P₄ > Cl₂ > Ar.

Electrical Conductivity

  • Increases Na → Al (more delocalized electrons). Si = semiconductor. P, S, Cl, Ar = zero.

Metallic Character & Redox Power

  • Metallic character DECREASES →. Reducing power DECREASES →. Oxidising power INCREASES →.

Electron Affinity — more negative → (exceptions: Mg positive; P less negative)

  • Mg: electron forced into less stable 3p from full 3s².
  • P: half-filled 3p³ is particularly stable — resists adding an electron.

14.2 — Chemical Properties

Reaction with Water

  • Na: 2Na + 2H₂O → 2NaOH + H₂  (vigorous, cold water)
  • Mg: Mg + H₂O → Mg(OH)₂ + H₂  (slow cold; fast steam → MgO + H₂)
  • Al: 2Al + 6H₂O → 2Al(OH)₃ + 3H₂  (very slow, steam only)
  • Si: Si + 2H₂O → SiO₂ + 2H₂  (steam, very high temp)
  • P, S: No reaction.  |  Cl: Cl₂ + H₂O → HCl + HOCl  (acidic solution)

Reaction with Oxygen

  • Na: 4Na + O₂ → 2Na₂O  |  Mg: 2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO  |  Al: 4Al + 3O₂ → 2Al₂O₃
  • Si: Si + O₂ → SiO₂  |  P: P₄ + 5O₂ → P₄O₁₀ (spontaneous)  |  S: S + O₂ → SO₂

Reaction with NaOH

  • Na, Mg: No reaction.
  • Al (amphoteric): 2Al + 2NaOH + 6H₂O → 2NaAl(OH)₄ + 3H₂
  • Cl: Cl₂ + 2NaOH → NaOCl + NaCl + H₂O  (NaOCl = bleach/Jik)

Reaction with HCl

  • Na: 2Na + 2HCl → 2NaCl + H₂  |  Mg: Mg + 2HCl → MgCl₂ + H₂  |  Al: 2Al + 6HCl → 2AlCl₃ + 3H₂
  • Si, P, S, Cl: No reaction with HCl.

FACTORS AFFECTING CHEMICAL EQUILIBRIUM

15.1 — Reversible vs. Irreversible Reactions

  • Irreversible: one direction only. E.g. NaOH + HCl → NaCl + H₂O  |  CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂
  • Reversible (⇌): both directions. E.g. 2SO₂ + O₂ ⇌ 2SO₃  |  PCl₅ ⇌ PCl₃ + Cl₂

15.2 — Dynamic Equilibrium

Definition
Equilibrium = state where rate of forward reaction = rate of backward reaction. Concentrations remain constant but reactions have NOT stopped.
  • Homogeneous: all species same phase. E.g. N₂(g) + O₂(g) ⇌ 2NO(g)
  • Heterogeneous: different phases. E.g. CaCO₃(s) ⇌ CaO(s) + CO₂(g)
  • Concentrations constant; system closed; temperature constant; can be reached from either direction.

15.3 — Le Châtelier's Principle

Principle
When equilibrium is disturbed, the system shifts in the direction that OPPOSES the change.

A) Temperature

  • Exothermic (ΔH < 0): ↑ temp → ← LEFT  |  ↓ temp → → RIGHT
  • Endothermic (ΔH > 0): ↑ temp → → RIGHT  |  ↓ temp → ← LEFT

B) Concentration

  • Add reactant → → RIGHT  |  Add product → ← LEFT
  • Remove product → → RIGHT  |  Remove reactant → ← LEFT

C) Pressure (gases only)

  • ↑ Pressure → shifts toward FEWER moles of gas.
  • ↓ Pressure → shifts toward MORE moles of gas.
  • Example: N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃ — 4 moles left, 2 right → ↑ pressure shifts → RIGHT.

D) Catalyst

  • Does NOT shift equilibrium. Speeds up BOTH reactions equally → equilibrium reached faster only.
  • Haber process: Iron (Fe).  |  Contact process: V₂O₅.
